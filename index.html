The Burmann family welcomes you.

We took over our bees from a beloved relative more than 20 years ago.

With no previous experience, it took a lot of books, the rest was learning by trial and error. The learning however never stops.

The beehives are spread all over the property. From two colonies directly under a lime tree, planted for the bees, to 5 colonies under the fruit trees and bees at the edge of the forest, we live with and for the bees.

A year with bees

The bees are busy collecting honey and when the summer is over, you can harvest. Right? Well, unfortunately no. The beekeeper is busy all year.

  • In winter, the beekeeper prepares frames for the honeycombs, repairs and cleans boxes or build new ones.
  • In spring the beekeeper will plant flowers to make sure, the bees have enough to eat.
  • In summer the beekeeper has to harvest the honey. But also clean the honeycombs, exchange broken ones and fill up the honey in glasses.
  • In fall the beekeeper has to make sure, his bees have enough food for the winter and are healthy. The beekeeper also has to check the bees for varoa mites.
